Understanding offshore revenue

Revenues are collected from offshore leases on Federal land in several ways. First, when a lease is first put to bid, the winning company pays a bonus. Once a company wins a lease, it has up to seven years to start production on that plot. During this time before production, the company pays rent. This is a fixed price-per-acre amount. Finally, once a lease starts producing, the company stops paying rent and instead starts paying royalties, which are calculated based on how much oil or gas the lease produces.

Understanding Our Disbursement Data

Mineral royalties collected by the federal government are disbursed to a variety of funds. 49 percent of onshore lease revenue goes to the state in which the lease is located (except in the case of Alaska, where 90 percent of the royalties go back to the state), 40 percent is disbursed to the national Reclamation Fund (except in the case of Alaska, where no Reclamation Fund disbursement takes place), and the remaining 10 percent goes to the U.S. Treasury. In the case of offshore leases, 27 percent of all payments go to the state in which lease is located, $150 million is deposited to the Historic Preservation Fund each year, and the remaining money goes to the U.S. Treasury. Source: taxfoundation.org.

This means that there are three funds that receive mineral royalty disbursements:

  • Historic Preservation Fund
  • Land & Water Conservation Fund
  • Reclamation Fund

And there are four groups of government that receive mineral royalty disbursements:

  • American Indian Tribes & Allottees
  • State Share: Offshore
  • State Share: Onshore
  • U.S. Treasury

Of those four groups, the two State Shares (Offshore and Onshore) are made up of further subdivisions because local jurisdictions receive portions.

State Share: Offshore consists of three disbursements:

  • 8(g)
  • GOMESA - Coastal Political Subdivision (CPS)
  • GOMESA - State
  • Detailed information on GOMESA disbursements available here.

State Share: Onshore consists of two disdursements:

  • Geothermal (which is disbursed at the county level)
  • State
